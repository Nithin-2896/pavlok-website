How Pavlok Works

Pavlok is not your typical wearable. Beyond tracking steps and sleep, it helps you break bad habits through real‑time feedback like vibrations, beeps, and gentle shocks.

How Does Pavlok Work?

Based on aversion therapy, Pavlok pairs negative feedback with undesired behaviors. For instance, triggering a light zap each time you bite your nails conditions your brain to associate the habit with discomfort.

The device syncs with a mobile app where you can schedule habits, track progress, and adjust feedback intensity—choosing between vibration, beep, or shock based on your personal goals.

What's Inside the Device?

Pavlok contains a battery, Bluetooth module, and a customizable feedback system. The mild zap is safe due to its low current, and its intensity can be adjusted from subtle reminders to more noticeable alerts.

How Effective is Pavlok?

Thousands of users have leveraged Pavlok to quit smoking, cut down on screen time, or beat procrastination. Its effectiveness lies in enhancing self-awareness and reinforcing behavior change.

Pavlok 2 – What's New?

The second-generation Pavlok features longer battery life, improved Bluetooth stability, better gesture detection, and refined shock calibration—making it even more efficient in helping you change habits.

Final Thoughts

Pavlok works by providing immediate, tailored feedback that disrupts negative behavior patterns. This real‑time intervention encourages you to break unwanted habits before they become ingrained.
